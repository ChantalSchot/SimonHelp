Background: More information on the (scientific) background of SiMON.


SiMON (SImulating Metabolism Of Nutrients in preruminants) is simulation software of a mechanistic model integrating protein and energy metabolism in preruminant calves of 80-240 kg live weight. Starting from the main menu, you can choose from the following 5 basic windows. Click on the window name for more detailed information. Although these windows will, after a while, be used in random order, they will be described from left to right. In all available windows, you can ask for help by clicking the help item in the main menu.
  • Genotype: Before you can perform a simulation, you will have to specify what genotype you would like to use in the simulation.

  • Feed: You can define different feed compositions in the feed window.

  • Schedules: Combinations of daily feeds and quantities given to the simulated animal can be specified in the schedules window.

  • Simulation: You can start a simulation in the simulation window, combining genotypes with feeding schedules and specify a desired initial live weight and length of the simulation (in days or in terms of target weight). You can specify up to 4 simulations in one go.

  • Output: Results for up to 4 simulations can be viewed in the output window. In the output windows, you can view results in a table or as a graph.
