Buttons

Copy
You can define a genotype by copying one of the three basic genotypes and make changes from there.

Delete
You can delete any genotype, except for the three basic genotypes.
Upon deletion of a genotype, simulations that make use of this genotype won't work properly.
You can change an existing genotype and save it.
Please realize that old simulations won't produce the same results after this.

Save
You can save your newly defined or changed genotype.

Restore
Undo changes since last save.

Print
Generate a report of the selected genotype for printing or exporting. The report is in HTML format.

Fields

Name
Choose a name for your genotype (max 15 characters)

Description
Choose an appropriate description

Based on
When defining a new genotype, you will see here which of the three basic genotypes your new one is based on.
This is important because the three basic genotypes differ in many parameters, of which you can see and change a few.

Maximum rate of amino acid oxidation
Change the default multiplier within the limits.
Increasing this factor increases the priority of amino acids for oxidation and will therefore decrease protein deposition and increase fat deposition rates.

Maximum rate of fat synthesis
Change the default multiplier within the limits.
Increasing this factor increases the priority of fatty acids for fat synthesis and therefore increases fat deposition rate and decrease the utilization of fatty acids for oxidation.

Maximum rate of fatty acid oxidation
Change the default multiplier within the limits.
Increasing this factor increases the priority of fatty acids to be used for oxidation (energy supply) and will lead to a decreased fat deposition and possibly saves oxidation of other nutrients.

Maximum rate of fatty acid synthesis
Change the default multiplier within the limits.
Increasing this factor increases the priority of acetyl coenzyme A to be used for fatty acid synthesis.
It will, indirectly, increase fat deposition and possibly a decreased protein deposition.

Energy requirement for maintenance processes
Change the default multiplier within the limits.
Increasing this factor increases energy expenditure on maintenance processes and will therefore reduce protein and fat deposition.
Please note that differences in maintenance energy expenditure between genotypes, as far as related to changes in body composition, are already accounted for by Simon.
Changing this multiplier is useful to account for differences in environment (e.g. ambient temperature, group housing systems, etc)

Functionality of the genotype window

In this window you can change the characteristics of three (basic) genotypes for which Simon is parameterized
(Dutch Black and White males and females and Dutch Red and White male calves), thus defining your own genotype.
By selecting a basic genotype, Simon copies its initial body composition and a complete set of parameters.
You can change five model parameters and thereby some of the nutrient partitioning rules that are used for your genotype.
These model parameters are multiplication factors and are set to 1 for the Dutch Black and White male calves.
If you copy a Dutch Red and White male calf as a basis to define your own genotype, the five multiplication factors will have a different default value.
Setting these to one will not give you a Dutch Black and White male calf, because these five parameters are not the only ones in which these genotypes differ.
It should be noted that Simon was originally calibrated for Dutch Black and White male calves, and the results obtained with this genotype are more reliable than with the other genotypes.
